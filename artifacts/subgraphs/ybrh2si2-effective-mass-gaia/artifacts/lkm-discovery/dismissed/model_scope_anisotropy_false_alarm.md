# Dismissed contradiction candidate: YbRh2Si2 anisotropy vs homogeneous isotropic model

Origin: limited LKM match search for `YbRh2Si2 anisotropy band structure effective mass`.

Candidate tension:

- Root leaf `gcn_03614e9b5933496a` says Shaginyan et al. 2010 use a homogeneous isotropic heavy-electron liquid model and neglect crystal-lattice anisotropy, Brillouin-zone structure, multiple bands, and anisotropic effective masses for universal scaling behavior in YbRh2Si2.
- LKM hits such as `gcn_c38f8ce989fd454a`, `gcn_42a4ff7fd004413f`, and `gcn_dd09c40e9184410a` report YbRh2Si2 Fermi-surface, band-structure, and ESR anisotropy details.

Decision: dismissed as a Gaia `contradiction()` because both can be true: the root claim is a scope-limited universal-scaling modeling approximation, while the search hits concern microscopic anisotropy or different observables/field regimes. This remains a synthesis suspicion: reviewers should ask how far the isotropic FCQPT scaling model can be pushed against anisotropic band-resolved data.
