# Dismissed contradiction: homogeneous isotropic FCQPT model vs material-specific RBC/Hall calculation

## Candidate tension

- Existing parent context: the YbRh2Si2 FCQPT branch includes a premise that models YbRh2Si2 and related heavy-fermion compounds as a homogeneous isotropic heavy-electron liquid, deliberately neglecting crystal-lattice anisotropy, Brillouin-zone structure, multiple bands, and anisotropic effective masses for universal scaling.
- New selected root: Friedemann et al. 2010 use a material-specific renormalized-band calculation with experimental CEF splittings, a thermodynamically calibrated 4f quasiparticle resonance width, band-resolved Hall transport products, and explicit YbRh2Si2/YbIr2Si2 DOS/gamma values.

## Verdict

Dismissed as a scope/suspicion note, not promoted to `contradiction()`.

## Rationale

The two claims can both be true: a homogeneous isotropic FCQPT model can be used as a universal scaling approximation while a renormalized-band calculation provides material-specific band/Hall/DOS evidence that the approximation omits. The new subgraph weakens the parent premise's generality and should qualify it during synthesis, but it does not logically exclude the parent model's stated universal-scaling use.

## Synthesis obligation

When this subgraph is merged into the parent package, add a scope-caution relation or synthesis claim rather than a contradiction unless future evidence states that the homogeneous isotropic FCQPT model fails for the same YbRh2Si2 observable under the same conditions.
